Tenant agnostic playbook that covers presales discovery, network readiness validation (CSAN), configuration workflow in Control Hub, migration and cutover, validation, and operations transition. Use this as the standard delivery reference and as the basis for handoff documentation.
Audience SA, PM, Implementation, Support

How we run implementations

Every deployment follows the same structure: presales discovery and readiness, design and build, validation, cutover, and a controlled transition to steady state support. Tenant details change, but the gates do not.

1
Presales discovery and readiness gate
SA confirms scope, dependencies, success criteria, and runs CSAN network testing before order and scheduling.
2
Design and build
Translate requirements into a dial plan, PSTN approach, user and device strategy, and feature configuration in Control Hub.
3
Validate and cutover
Confirm call flows, E911, number routing, devices, and quality. Execute change plan and perform controlled cutover.
4
Hypercare and operations transition
Short, structured hypercare with acceptance criteria, then handoff to support with final documentation and monitoring baselines.

Presales

The Solution Architect owns presales discovery and sets the delivery team up for success. The goal is to remove ambiguity early: what is being delivered, what success looks like, what dependencies exist, and what could cause delays or quality issues.

Presales topic What the SA confirms Output
Business drivers Why the customer is changing, desired end state, and the adoption approach for users and admins. Success criteria, acceptance criteria, go live definition.
Scope boundaries What is in scope and what is out of scope: sites, user groups, call flows, devices, integrations. Implementation scope statement and assumptions list.
PSTN approach Calling plans vs Local Gateway vs integrated PSTN provider model, number porting needs, emergency calling requirements. PSTN decision and prerequisites for delivery scheduling.
Dial plan and routing Extensions, E.164 strategy, site codes, legacy patterns, call permissions, and any interop needs. Dial plan design inputs for build.
Devices and endpoints Desk phones, soft clients, room devices, paging, analog, ATA needs, and provisioning model. Endpoint BOM and provisioning approach.
Network readiness LAN/WAN, QoS posture, firewall policy, NAT, proxy, and voice quality baseline. CSAN results plus remediation actions.
Security and identity SSO status, MFA posture, admin roles, access model, and operational ownership boundaries. Identity readiness notes and admin matrix.
Project logistics Stakeholders, change windows, blackout periods, and escalation paths. Project schedule constraints and communications plan.

Network readiness gate: CSAN

During presales we run CSAN to validate network and firewall readiness, and to reduce the risk of cutover day issues. CSAN should be treated as a gate: if results show risk, remediation is planned before implementation scheduling.

When
Presales, before final scope signoff and scheduling
Who runs it
SA or implementation engineer with customer network contact
What to capture
Run date, locations tested, summary results, and remediation items
Evidence
CSAN output exported or summarized in the handoff document
Tip: keep CSAN results paired with a short remediation plan and a retest date so the project does not stall.

Presales risks to call out early

These are common root causes of delays or degraded voice quality when discovered late.

Firewall policy unknown or unmanaged Customer cannot confirm outbound policy, proxy behavior, or NAT impacts for real time media.
No QoS posture Voice packets are not prioritized end to end. Validate LAN tagging and WAN queuing early.
Number porting timeline risk Ports need LOA, CSR validation, carrier lead times, and a backout plan if the port window slips.
Legacy analog and paging complexity Analog lines, elevators, alarms, and paging often require site surveys and lab validation.

Implementation workflow

The implementation phase converts the approved design into configured services, tested call flows, and a controlled go live. The sequence below mirrors the common Control Hub based configuration flow and feature setup approach.

1
Confirm prerequisites and identity readiness
Validate access to Control Hub, admin roles, identity strategy (local vs SSO), and baseline policies. Confirm who owns day two operations and what tasks will be handed to support.
2
Define locations and core calling settings
Create locations, assign calling behavior, emergency calling assumptions per location, and any location level settings needed for consistent routing.
3
Select and implement PSTN connectivity
Implement the agreed PSTN model (Calling Plans, Local Gateway, or provider integrated approach), confirm number management plan, and validate inbound and outbound routing.
4
Build dial plan and call permissions
Implement extension ranges, E.164 strategy, site codes if needed, normalization rules, and class of service and call permissions.
5
Provision users and endpoints
Add users, assign licenses, assign numbers and extensions, provision devices, confirm activation workflow, and validate client sign in experience.
6
Configure calling features and call flows
Configure hunt groups, call queues, auto attendants, receptionist client where applicable, and shared line behavior. Validate business hour logic and after hours routing.
7
Validation and acceptance testing
Execute test plan across inbound, outbound, internal calling, voicemail, E911, feature flows, and endpoint behavior. Capture results and remediation actions.
8
Cutover, hypercare, and operations transition
Execute change plan in approved window, confirm acceptance criteria, provide hypercare coverage, then hand off with completed documentation and monitoring baselines.

Validation checklist

Use this checklist during build validation and again during go live validation. Capture evidence for every critical call flow.

Inbound callingDID routes correctly, correct AA or queue, correct business hours and after hours behavior.
Outbound callingCorrect caller ID, correct permissions, correct routing, no unexpected blocking, international policy aligns to design.
Internal and extension dialingExtensions reach intended user, shared lines work as expected, pick up and park behavior matches design.
Voicemail and greetingsVoicemail deposit and retrieval, greetings, MWI on endpoints, and notification behavior.
Emergency callingE911 behavior validated per location where possible, address accuracy confirmed, test call procedure documented.
Quality and media pathTwo way audio, DTMF, hold music, transfers, conference, and no unexpected one way audio across sites.

Cutover and backout

Cutover success depends on predictable execution and clear rollback criteria. Use controlled decision points.

Change window
Approved window with stakeholders available and escalation contacts on hand
Cutover method
Port event, trunk flip, routing change, or phased user migration
Backout trigger
If critical flows fail and cannot be remediated within agreed time
Backout method
Restore prior routing, restore trunks, revert number routing, restore device registration plan
Acceptance criteria
Top call flows pass, E911 addressed, core users can place and receive calls reliably
Use decision gates Document evidence
